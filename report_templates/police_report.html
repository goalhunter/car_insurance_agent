NARRATIVE / OFFICER'S STATEMENT
On October 3, 2025, at approximately 1435 hours, I, Officer Patricia Collins (Badge #2847), responded to a traffic collision at the intersection of Commercial Boulevard and NE 25th Avenue in Fort Lauderdale, Florida.

Upon arrival, I observed two vehicles involved in a side-impact collision. Vehicle #1, a silver 2019 Honda Accord operated by Carlos Martinez, and Vehicle #2, a silver 2022 Toyota RAV4 Hybrid operated by Michael Johnson.

Investigation revealed that Vehicle #1 (Martinez) was traveling eastbound on Commercial Boulevard and failed to stop at the red traffic signal at the intersection with NE 25th Avenue. Vehicle #2 (Johnson) was traveling northbound on NE 25th Avenue and had the right of way with a green traffic signal.

Vehicle #1 struck Vehicle #2 on the driver's side front quarter panel and door area, causing significant damage to Vehicle #2's left front fender, door, and bumper assembly. The impact caused Vehicle #2 to spin approximately 45 degrees clockwise before coming to rest in the intersection.

Both drivers were wearing seat belts at the time of collision. Neither driver reported injuries and both declined medical attention at the scene. Both vehicles were drivable and moved to the parking lot at 2450 Commercial Boulevard to complete the investigation.

Mr. Martinez stated he was distracted by his GPS navigation system and did not observe the red traffic signal. Two independent witnesses, Maria Santos and Robert Williams, confirmed that Vehicle #1 ran the red light and was at fault for the collision. Their contact information is on file.

Mr. Johnson stated he was proceeding through the intersection on a green light when Vehicle #1 struck his vehicle. His account is consistent with witness statements and physical evidence at the scene.

Weather conditions were overcast but dry. Road surface was in good condition with no contributing factors. Traffic signal was operational and functioning properly, confirmed by witness testimony.

Vehicle #1 sustained minor damage to the front bumper and hood. Vehicle #2 sustained moderate to severe damage to the driver's side front quarter panel, fender, door, bumper, and associated components.

Based on investigation, physical evidence, and witness statements, Vehicle #1 (Martinez) was determined to be 100% at fault for this collision due to failure to yield right of way at a controlled intersection (red traffic signal violation). Citation #TC-2025-84729 was issued to Mr. Martinez for Failure to Yield Right of Way (Florida Statute 316.075(1)(a)).

No DUI suspected. No evidence of reckless driving. Scene photographs taken and filed with this report. Both parties exchanged insurance information. Report completed at scene.
